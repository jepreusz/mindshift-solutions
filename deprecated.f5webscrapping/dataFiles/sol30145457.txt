


Known IssueAttempts to load a configuration may fail after a peer is removed from a Sync-Only device group.This issue occurs when all of the following conditions are met:Your BIG-IP APM configuration uses a Sync-Only device group.Your system synchronizes to the device group.You delete a device from the Sync-Only device group that has not been previously synchronized to the device group.You attempt to load the configuration on the device that was deleted from the device group.ImpactThe configuration fails to load.SymptomsAs a result of this issue, you may encounter the following symptom:When attempting to load the configuration, error messages similar to the following example are displayed:		Validating configuration...	  /config/bigip_base.conf	  /config/bigip_user.conf	  /config/bigip.conf	01070712:3: Values (/<partition>/<peer device>) specified for Policy Sync Status (/<partition>/<policy sync name>/<peer device> /<partition>/<peer device>): foreign key index (device_FK) do not point at an item that exists in the database.	Unexpected Error: Validating configuration process failed.ResolutionStatusF5 Product Development has assigned ID 475735 to this issue. F5 has confirmed that this issue exists in the products listed in the Applies To box, located in the upper-right corner of this article. For information about releases or hotfixes that resolve this issue, refer to the following table:Type of FixFixes Introduced InRelated ArticlesRelease12.0.0			11.5.3SOL2200: Most recent versions of F5 softwareHotfix11.6.0 HF6SOL9502: BIG-IP hotfix matrixWorkaroundTo work around this issue, you can remove the peer device from the Sync-Only device group on which the previous policy synchronization was performed.Impact of workaround: Performing the suggested workaround should not have a negative impact on your system.Supplemental InformationSOL4918: Overview of the F5 critical issue hotfix policySOL167: Downloading software and firmware from F5SOL13123: Managing BIG-IP product hotfixes (11.x - 12.x)
